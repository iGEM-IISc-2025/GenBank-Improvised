What is GenBank-Improvised


About Us — GenBank-Improved

We are a student team from the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, participating in the iGEM (International Genetically Engineered Machine) competition. Our project grew from a simple observation: many biologists and researchers face hurdles when navigating sequence repositories like GenBank — complex interfaces, steep learning curves, and difficulty retrieving or analyzing data.

To address these issues, we designed an improved, user-centered GenBank interface that bridges the gap between powerful bioinformatics resources and modern usability.

What We Offer

  • Improved Search and Navigation — Streamlined tools to help you quickly find the sequences you need.
  • Interactive Visualizations — Graphical representations to make sequence features easier to understand.
  • Curated Insights — Highlighted annotations, summaries, and links to related studies.
  • Simplified Design — A clean, modern interface that reduces complexity without losing scientific depth.

Our Mission

Our goal is to make biological databases more accessible, efficient, and inclusive. Science should not be limited by technical barriers—every user, from undergraduate students to experienced researchers, should be able to explore sequence data with confidence and ease.

Acknowledgments

This initiative is part of our iGEM project at IISc Bangalore. We thank our mentors, collaborators, and the wider iGEM community for their guidance and support.
